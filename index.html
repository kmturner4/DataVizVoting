Here is my Parallel Categories Plot:


This visualization shows the relationship of voters by three categories of voting frequency with the voter's educational attainment category and their income category. About two-thirds of people who always vote attended college, with around half of ‘always’ voters finishing college. Of those who finished college, the majority fell into the two higher-income categories. Sporadic voters were roughlysplit into the three educational categories and the four income groups. People who rarely or never voted were most likely to fall in the income category of less than $40,000. People in this voter group were least likely to fall into the highest income category. They were also most likely to have only a high school education. Generally, those who voted more frequently were more likely to have a higher level of education and fall into a higher income category.


Here is my Bubble Chart:

This bubble chart illustrates the relationship between the percent of active registered voters who voted in that election and the percent of votes that were cast by absentee ballots. Additonally, the region of each data point, representing a county in Virginia, corresponds to a color. The size of the circle indicates the total number of registered voters in that county. There is a different visualization for each election year, conveying the differences across the years. For example, 2020 has a very high rate of absentee ballots, driven by the COVID-19 pandemic. Additonally, in years of presidential elections the perecent of active registered voters who voted was higher than for years with only gubernational elections.


Here is my Choropleth Map:

This visualization offers a different perspective on the same data from the bubble chart, although this map only includes data from the 2022 general election. In the map, each county is shaded by the percent of voter turnout. Southwest Virginia had the lowest rates of voting in the 2022 election. Generally, there is not a strong pattern for the rest of the state, although there are higher rates in the northern and central parts of the state. Some parts of Hampton Roads were higher than the rest of the surrounding counties, as was one county in the west-central part of the state and a few in the central and northern-central parts of the state.
